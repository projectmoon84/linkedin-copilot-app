# Post composing guidance

This document defines how the LinkedIn Copilot app helps users write, hook, and repurpose content. It covers three areas: writing a post from scratch, crafting a hook, and repurposing existing content into new formats.

---

## Part 1: Writing a LinkedIn post

### What to collect before writing

Before generating any post, gather the following from the user:

1. **Topic or core idea** — what is this post about?
2. **Target audience** — who is reading this?
3. **Key insight, story, or data point** — the thing that makes it worth reading
4. **Post framework** — choose one (or recommend based on the topic):
   - **Story** — personal narrative leading to a business lesson
   - **Framework** — a step-by-step breakdown the reader can apply
   - **Hot take** — contrarian opinion → proof → lesson
   - **Case study** — specific results with real data
   - **Behind the scenes** — the process or "messy middle" reveal
5. **CTA type** — one of:
   - Question (drives comments and engagement)
   - Resource (offer something via DM with a keyword)
   - Soft follow (encourages following for more)

---

### Post structure: the epiphany bridge

Every post follows this six-part arc:

**1. Hook (lines 1–2)**
The scroll-stopper. See Part 2 for hook frameworks. This is the most important line — if it fails, nothing else matters.

**2. Agitation (lines 3–6)**
Validate the reader's pain. Make them think "that's me." Be visceral and specific. Not "Sales is hard." Instead: "You spent four hours in the DMs and have £0 to show for it."

**3. The shift (lines 7–12)**
Introduce the new way. Discredit the old way. Old way = what everyone else does. New way = the user's unique insight or framework.

**4. Evidence (lines 13–18)**
A specific result, case study, or data point. Moves the post from opinion to fact. Use exact numbers: "We added £42,000 to the pipeline in 11 days."

**5. Takeaway (lines 19–24)**
Three to five actionable bullets using → arrows. The "save-able" part of the post. Each bullet must stand alone as useful advice.

**6. CTA (final lines)**
One ask only. Never stack multiple CTAs.
- Question: "What's your experience with X?"
- Resource: "I put together a free [X]. Comment [KEYWORD] and I'll DM it."
- Soft follow: "Follow me for more frameworks like this."

---

### Formatting rules (non-negotiable)

- Maximum 250 words total
- No paragraph longer than two lines
- Blank line between every sentence or section
- Use → for bullet points — never numbered lists in the post body
- No hashtags in the body (three max at the very end only, if needed)
- No emojis
- No external links in the post body (these kill organic reach significantly)
- No filler transitions: no "In conclusion," "Furthermore," "Moreover"

---

### Banned vocabulary

Never use any of the following words or phrases in a post:

delve, landscape, elevate, unlock, unleash, tapestry, game-changer, foster, utilise, leverage, synergy, in today's world, I'm excited to share, let's talk about

---

### Self-check before delivering a post

Before showing the user a draft, verify:

- [ ] Hook is under 200 characters and stops the scroll
- [ ] No paragraph exceeds two lines
- [ ] Every sentence passes the bar test (would you say this in conversation?)
- [ ] Post is under 250 words
- [ ] Zero banned vocabulary used
- [ ] CTA is clear and singular
- [ ] The post teaches, proves, or directs — it doesn't just entertain

## Part 2: Writing a hook

### What to collect before generating hooks

1. **Topic or core idea**
2. **Target audience** — who is this for?
3. **The "so what?"** — why should the reader care right now?
4. **Any specific data, results, or stories** to reference

Do not generate hooks until all four are known.

---

### Hook frameworks

Generate 10 hooks using a mix of these four frameworks:

**1. The contrarian (pattern interrupt)**
State the opposite of what the audience believes.
- "[Common belief] is wrong. Here's why."
- "Stop doing [popular tactic]. It's costing you [specific loss]."

**2. The specificity hook (data-backed)**
Use precise numbers — the brain trusts specific data.
- "I [action] [exact number] [things]. [Unexpected result]."
- "[Exact metric] in [exact timeframe]. Here's the system."

**3. The negative bias (loss aversion)**
Focus on mistakes, failures, or hidden danger.
- "The [topic] mistake costing you [specific loss]."
- "If you're still doing [old way], read this."

**4. The story opener (in media res)**
Start in the middle of the action with no setup.
- "[Dramatic moment]. [One-line context]."
- "I stared at [specific thing]. [Unexpected observation]."

---

### Hook rules

1. No questions as openers ("Did you know?" or "Are you struggling?")
2. No label-style openers ("Here's how to..." or "5 tips for...")
3. Each hook must be under 200 characters — one line on mobile
4. No hashtags
5. No emojis
6. No "I'm excited to share" or any variation
7. Opening sentence must be under 12 words
8. Must sound like a real person, not a press release

---

### How to present hooks to the user

Show all 10 in a numbered list. After each hook, note which framework it uses.

Example format:
1. "I sent 1,000 cold emails. 998 were ignored." (Specificity)
2. "Your hiring process is designed to fail." (Contrarian)

Then ask: **"Which one raises your eyebrows?"**

- Options 1–3 are usually the "safe" choices — push the user past them
- Options 4–8 are the sweet spot
- If a hook feels risky, it's probably the right one
- If a hook feels safe, it should be discarded

---

### Self-check before delivering hooks

- [ ] Every hook is under 200 characters
- [ ] Each would make someone stop scrolling
- [ ] Each creates a curiosity gap or emotional spike
- [ ] Zero banned words used
- [ ] Each sounds like a real person, not a marketer

## Part 3: Repurposing content

### What to collect before repurposing

1. **The original content** — full text pasted in
2. **Performance data if available** — impressions, comments, saves
3. **Target formats** — which of these to create:
   - LinkedIn (new angle)
   - Twitter / X thread
   - Email newsletter snippet
   - Carousel outline
   - Short-form video script (60 seconds)
   - Quote cards (3–5)
4. **Brand voice or tone guidelines** (if available)
5. **Stagger timeline preference** — 1 week, 2 weeks, or 4 weeks

---

### Phase 1: Extract the core content

Before writing any derivative, extract from the original:
- The core thesis (one sentence)
- Three to five key insights or talking points
- The strongest data point or story
- The most quotable line
- The framework or structure (if any)

---

### Phase 2: Create the derivatives

**LinkedIn (new angle)**
- Completely different hook
- Same core insight, different entry point
- New framework or structure (if original was a story, make this a list)
- Safe to post two to four weeks after the original

**Twitter / X thread**
- Hook tweet: standalone, under 280 characters, creates curiosity
- Five to eight body tweets: one key insight per tweet, each must stand alone
- Final tweet: CTA linking to newsletter, profile, or resource
- No hashtags in the thread body

**Email newsletter snippet**
- Three subject line options (curiosity / benefit / urgency)
- 150–250 words expanding on the strongest insight
- More personal and intimate in tone than social media
- One CTA at the end

**Carousel outline**
- Cover slide: hook from the original post, rewritten for visual format
- Six to eight content slides: one insight per slide, max 25 words each
- Final slide: follow, save, or comment CTA

**Short-form video script (60 seconds)**
- Hook (first 3 seconds): text on screen + spoken line
- Body (45 seconds): three key points delivered conversationally
- CTA (final 12 seconds): what to do next
- Include brief visual suggestions for each section

**Quote cards (3–5)**
- Extract the most quotable lines from the original
- Format: "[Quote]" — [Name]
- Note recommended dimensions: 1080×1080 or 1080×1350

---

### Phase 3: Distribution timeline

Provide a staggered publishing schedule using this pattern:

| Day | Platform | Format | Notes |
|-----|----------|--------|-------|
| Day 1 | LinkedIn | Original post | — |
| Day 3 | Twitter / X | Thread version | Different hook |
| Day 5 | Email | Newsletter snippet | Send to list |
| Day 8 | LinkedIn | Carousel version | Visual format |
| Day 12 | Instagram / TikTok | Video script | Record and post |
| Day 15 | LinkedIn | Quote card series | One per day for three days |

Adjust the schedule to match the user's chosen stagger timeline.

---

### Repurposing rules

- Never copy-paste the same text across platforms
- Every derivative must have a platform-native hook
- Rewrite the hook for every format — the hook is 90% of the battle
- Respect platform-specific constraints (tweet length, video duration, etc.)
- Each derivative must stand alone — a reader shouldn't need to see the original

---

### Self-check before delivering repurposed content

- [ ] Every derivative has a unique, platform-specific hook
- [ ] No copy-paste duplication across formats
- [ ] Each piece stands alone without context from the original
- [ ] Distribution timeline is staggered, not clustered on one day
- [ ] Quote cards are punchy and under 15 words
